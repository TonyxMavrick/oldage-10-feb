Privacy Policy

Your privacy and the confidentiality of your information are our highest priorities

Last Updated: February 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how GentleHandsCare collects, uses, protects, and shares your personal information.

1. Information We Collect

To provide compassionate, personalized care, we collect various types of information:

Personal Identification Information

  • Name, address, date of birth
  • Phone number and email address
  • Social Security number (when required for billing or insurance)
  • Emergency contact information
  • Payment and billing information

Health and Medical Information

  • Medical history and current health conditions
  • Medications and allergies
  • Physical and cognitive abilities
  • Dietary restrictions and preferences
  • Physician and healthcare provider information
  • Care preferences and daily routines

Service-Related Information

  • Care plans and service agreements
  • Caregiver notes and observations
  • Communication records (emails, calls, messages)
  • Service schedules and visit logs

Website Usage Information

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Referral sources and navigation patterns
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

Service Delivery

  • Creating and implementing personalized care plans
  • Matching clients with appropriate caregivers
  • Coordinating care schedules and services
  • Monitoring care quality and client well-being
  • Communicating about service updates and changes

Business Operations

  • Processing payments and managing billing
  • Maintaining accurate records and documentation
  • Training and supervising caregivers
  • Improving our services and operations
  • Conducting quality assurance and satisfaction surveys

Legal and Safety Requirements

  • Complying with healthcare regulations and laws
  • Protecting the safety of clients and caregivers
  • Responding to legal requests and court orders
  • Preventing fraud and ensuring security

Communication

  • Responding to inquiries and requests
  • Sending service confirmations and reminders
  • Providing updates about our services (with your consent)
  • Soliciting feedback and testimonials

3. Data Protection and Confidentiality

HIPAA Compliance: While GentleHandsCare provides non-medical home care services, we adhere to HIPAA-equivalent privacy standards to protect all health-related information with the highest level of confidentiality and security.

Security Measures

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

  • Physical Security: Locked file cabinets, restricted access areas, secure document disposal
  • Technical Security: Encrypted data transmission, secure servers, firewall protection, regular security audits
  • Administrative Security: Staff training on privacy protocols, confidentiality agreements, access controls
  • Monitoring: Regular security assessments and incident response procedures

Employee Training

All employees, caregivers, and contractors receive comprehensive training on:

  • Privacy laws and regulations
  • Confidentiality requirements
  • Proper handling of sensitive information
  • Data breach prevention and response

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information only in the following circumstances:

With Your Consent

  • Sharing updates with family members you designate
  • Coordinating with your healthcare providers (with authorization)
  • Using testimonials (only with explicit written permission)

Service Providers

  • Caregivers assigned to your care (limited to necessary information)
  • Billing and payment processors (for financial transactions)
  • IT and security service providers (under strict confidentiality agreements)

Legal Requirements

  • Compliance with court orders or subpoenas
  • Reporting suspected abuse or neglect (as mandated by law)
  • Protecting against fraud or illegal activity
  • Emergency situations to protect health and safety

Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred to the new entity, subject to the same privacy protections outlined in this policy.

5. Cookies and Analytics

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to improve user experience and analyze website traffic.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may limit website functionality.

6. Third-Party Service Providers

We carefully select third-party service providers and require them to:

  • Sign comprehensive confidentiality agreements
  • Implement appropriate security measures
  • Use information only for specified purposes
  • Comply with applicable privacy laws
  • Report any security incidents immediately

Our website may contain links to external sites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party websites.

7. Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Access

Request copies of your personal information

Correction

Request corrections to inaccurate information

Deletion

Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)

Restriction

Request limitations on how we use your information

Portability

Receive your information in a portable format

Opt-Out

Withdraw consent for marketing communications

To exercise these rights, please contact our Privacy Officer at care@GentleHandsCare.in or call +1-930-434-5498.

8. Children's and Senior Privacy Protection

Our website and services are not directed to individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors without parental consent.

Senior Privacy Protections

We take extra care to protect the privacy of seniors, especially those with cognitive impairments:

  • Clear, simple consent processes
  • Family involvement in privacy decisions (when authorized)
  • Regular privacy preference confirmations
  • Special protections for vulnerable adults

9. Data Retention

We retain your information only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy and as required by law:

  • Active Client Records: Maintained during active service and for 7 years after service ends
  • Financial Records: Retained for 7 years as required by tax and accounting regulations
  • Medical Information: Retained according to state healthcare record retention laws
  • Marketing Data: Deleted upon request or after 3 years of inactivity

After retention periods expire, we securely destroy or anonymize information to prevent unauthorized access.

10. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website with a new "Last Updated" date
  • Sending email notifications to active clients
  • Providing written notice with service documentation
  • Requesting renewed consent when required by law

We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
